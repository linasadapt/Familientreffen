About Druskininkai & Lithuania

A small spa town in southern Lithuania, surrounded by pine forest and three small lakes. Quiet, walkable, and easy on small kids and grandparents alike.

54.0°N 23.9°E ~13,000 residents 130 km south of Vilnius Since 1794
At a glance

Lithuania’s oldest spa town, woven through with pine.

Druskininkai is Lithuania’s oldest spa resort — known since the 19th century for its mineral springs. The old town is full of wooden villas, the new town wraps around Druskonis Lake, and pine forest threads through everything.

Getting there

Three sensible ways in.

Druskininkai is in southern Lithuania, about 130 km from Vilnius and 130 km from Kaunas. There’s no airport in town — most people fly into Vilnius or Kaunas and continue by car or bus.

By air

Vilnius (VNO) or Kaunas (KUN)

1 h 45 min onward by car

Vilnius (VNO) is the main airport. Kaunas (KUN) is a similar drive. Warsaw Modlin/Chopin (Poland) is also an option — about 4 h 30 min driving from there.

By car

Straight south on the A4

~1 h 45 min from Vilnius

Driving from Vilnius is the most flexible — straight south on the A4. Druskininkai has plenty of free street parking, and the town centre is small enough to walk.

By bus

Daily coaches from Vilnius

~2 h direct

Daily coaches connect Vilnius and Druskininkai (~2 h). Less convenient with luggage and small kids, but workable — and very cheap.
